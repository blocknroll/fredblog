First Day Paragliding

By Fred Block

4/27/2019

From the first time I saw one, I knew I wanted to fly paragliders. And now I'm finally here, doing it. I'm at Point of the Mountain in Salt Lake City, Utah – its considered one of the best spots in the world to learn and its only 25 minutes south of the city. Its a beautiful day.

I'm super excited to meet my new instructor, the one and only Chris Santacroce. Santa! Chris is a legend in the paragliding world, and no matter how much I can write right now, I don't have time to tell you about all the amazingly cool things he's done. Amongst many other things, Chris flew in the Red Bull Air Force as a professional adventurer – and those stories alone need their own dedicated blog...

There's an interesting pace to the scene. Its all very easy-going, as you'd expect, but when the conditions are right, everyone steps into a different sort of gear and you can feel the pace change. Time to set up the gear.

Chris is already working his magic with his student, Caspar. This is Caspar's first flight off the top of the south side and this is a pretty big moment. I'm lucky to be here. Caspar's stories also need their own blog – he's an awesome dude.

Time to launch. I was too new to be of any help, so I figured I'd stay out of the way and take some shots to document the big moment. Much to learn...
